Tectonic Setting

Kurdistan lies within the Zagros fold-thrust belt, formed by the collision of the Arabian and Eurasian plates.

Stratigraphy

The region contains a rich succession of Paleozoic, Mesozoic, and Cenozoic sedimentary rocks.

Petroleum Systems

Kurdistan has abundant hydrocarbon resources, especially in folded and faulted traps.

Structural Geology

Features include thrust faults, anticlines, synclines, and fault-related folds.

Economic Geology

The region hosts minerals like gypsum, limestone, and bitumen.

Geological Hazards

Earthquakes and landslides are significant hazards in the region.
